# The Holy Quran — Comprehensive Summary

## Structure Overview

The Quran consists of **114 surahs (chapters)** with a total of **6,236 verses**. Chapters are arranged roughly by length (longest to shortest), not chronologically. They are classified as **Meccan** (revealed before the migration to Medina — focused on faith, monotheism, afterlife) or **Medinan** (revealed after — focused on law, community, and governance).

## Major Themes

### 1. Tawheed (Monotheism)
The central theme: God is One, without partners, the Creator and Sustainer of all.

### 2. Prophethood
Stories of 25+ prophets (Adam, Nuh, Ibrahim, Musa, Isa, Muhammad) showing a continuous message.

### 3. Day of Judgment
Vivid descriptions of resurrection, accountability, Paradise and Hellfire.

### 4. Moral Guidance
Justice, charity, honesty, patience, gratitude, humility, kindness to parents and orphans.

### 5. Legal Rulings
Marriage, divorce, inheritance, commerce, dietary laws, criminal justice (primarily in Medinan surahs).

### 6. Stories of Past Nations
'Ad, Thamud, Pharaoh's people — destroyed for rejecting God's messengers.

### 7. Nature as Signs
Creation of heavens, earth, rain, animals, day/night cycles as evidence of God.

### 8. Social Justice
Care for orphans, the poor, freeing slaves, condemning exploitation.

### 9. Human Nature
Impatience, ingratitude, arrogance contrasted with faith, patience, and submission to God.

### 10. The Quran Itself
Self-referential: described as guidance, light, mercy, criterion, and a challenge to produce anything like it.
